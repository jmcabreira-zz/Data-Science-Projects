In this video we will look at the p-Value for Statistical Hypothesis used in statistics for data science using R programming language
Download the dataset used in the video: http://softlect.in/datasets/ZominosCh...

Descriptive statistics can help us identify relationships in data. Descriptive statistics do not draw a conclusion beyond the data samples. Inferential statistics does allow us to make conclusions beyond the data sample that is the population. Inferential statistics is the process of drawing conclusions about the population based on a sample taken from the population. The sample is the data obtained from the population.

Understanding the p-value:
The p-value is the probability that if the null hypothesis is true, sampling variation would produce an estimate. The p-value tells us how likely it is to get a result like this if the null hypothesis is true.
